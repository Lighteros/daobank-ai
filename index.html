FAQ

What is DaoBank?

DaoBank is a decentralized borrowing protocol that allows you to draw interest-free loans against $wDAO used as collateral. Loans are facilitated in $daoUSD (a low-volatility stablecoin soft-pegged to the US dollar) and need to maintain an overcollateralized ratio. DaoBank as a protocol is non-custodial, immutable, and governance-free. Learn more in our documentation

Why DaoBank?

DaoBank was born as a response to the call for asset holders in the Bittensor community to unlock liquidity for their $DAO tokens without necessarily selling them. Their need for DeFI products and solutions around $DAO is the overarching inspiration behind DaoBank

How do I interact with DaoBank protocol?

In order to interact with DaoBank protocol, you simply supply your $DAO ($wDAO) and amount. After supplying, you can borrow against your supplied $wDAO up to 60% LTV

What is the cost of interacting with DaoBank?

Interacting with the protocol requires transactions and so transaction fees for Ethereum Blockchain usage, which depend on the network status and transaction complexity.

Where is my collateral kept?

Your collateral is kept in the vault. The Vault maintains two balances: one is the asset ($wDAO tokens) acting as collateral and the other is a debt denominated in $daoUSD.

Is there any risk?

No platform can be considered entirely risk free. The risks related to the DaoBank platform are the smart contract risk (risk of a bug within the protocol code) and liquidation risk (risk on the collateral liquidation process). Every possible step has been taken to minimise the risk as much as possible-- the protocol code is public and open source. Additionally, DaoBank will launch a bug bounty campaign.

$DBANK token

$DBANK is used as the centre of gravity of the DaoBank protocol governance. $DBANK is used to vote and decide on the outcome of the protocol. Apart from this, $DBANK can be staked within the protocol to provide security to the protocol/suppliers. Stakers will earn staking rewards and fees from the protocol. Documentation on tokenomics and governance is available in the documentation If you are unsure about any specific terms feel free to ask any team member or moderator in the community chats

How does DaoBank handle redemptions?

Redemption is an action of depositing $daoUSD into the Vaults of other users and withdrawing the equivalent value of collateral. Redemptions take place when a profit can be made by buying $daoUSD below peg to acquire collateral at $1 per $daoUSD. When $daoUSD is redeemed for $wDAO, the system cancels the $daoUSD debt from Vaults, and the collateral asset is drawn from their collateral. Protocol owned whitelisted addresses execute redemptions to prevent any exit of funds from the protocol. Profits from redemptions will be used to increase the liquidity for $DBANK and $daoUSD.

How does DaoBank handle liquidations?

In case of liquidations, Vaults are offered on a Dutch auction where bidders can buy the collateral assets for $daoUSD. The initial price of the Vault is set to the value of the collateral. Then, the price gradually drops over the specified time period (voted via governance), enabling the bidders to realize an immediate arbitrage gain as the value of the Vault debt is usually expected to be lower than the value of the collateral assets. If there are no bidders during the Dutch Auction, the auction is canceled and the liquidation is handled by the protocol.
